## SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market

**US-based freelancers and small businesses who actively manage client testimonials and have SaaS budget.**

### Freelancer Segment
- US freelancers: ~76M
- % who actively collect testimonials: ~15-25% (most just let them happen organically)

- % with SaaS budget for this category: ~20% (many use free tools/manual methods)
- Active SaaS buyers: **3M freelancers**
- At blended $12/mo (mix of free and Pro): **$432M/year**

### Small Business / Agency Segment
- US small businesses (<50 employees): ~6M employer firms
- % in services (where testimonials matter most): ~60% → 3.6M
- % actively collecting testimonials: ~30% → 1.08M
- % willing to pay for tooling: ~25% → 270K
- At blended $25/mo (higher tier usage): **$81M/year**

### Solopreneur Segment
- US nonemployer businesses: ~33M
- Overlap with freelancers is significant (~70%)
- Net new (not counted in freelancer segment): ~10M
- % managing testimonials: ~10% → 1M
- At blended $10/mo: **$120M/year**

**SAM: ~$630M/year**

## Market Tailwinds

1. **Freelancer growth**: 64M → 86M+ by 2027 (US alone). Market is expanding fast.
2. **AI cost deflation**: LLM API costs dropping 10x/year. Our COGS on generation will decrease rapidly.
3. **Social proof emphasis**: 92% of consumers read reviews. Businesses know testimonials matter — they just lack tools.
4. **ChatGPT normalization**: People expect AI to do this kind of work now. Adoption friction is low.

## Market Headwinds

1. **Low willingness to pay**: Freelancers are notoriously cost-sensitive. Free tier is table stakes.
2. **Feature copyability**: Testimonial.to could add AI generation in 6 months. Speed to market is critical.
3. **Niche ceiling**: This may top out at a "lifestyle business" ($500K-1M ARR) rather than a VC-scale opportunity. **That's fine for us** — the ventures portfolio targets lifestyle-scale businesses.

---

## Bottom Line

ProofKit targets a **$630M SAM** with strong tailwinds (freelancer growth, AI adoption, social proof emphasis). Year 1 target of **$22-54K ARR** is achievable with content-led organic growth and a compelling free tier. The market is big enough to be worthwhile but niche enough that we won't attract VC-backed competitors immediately.

**Risk-adjusted confidence: 7/10** — Strong demand signals, validated pricing, but execution risk on user acquisition.
