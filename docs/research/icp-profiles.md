# ProofKit — Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

*Research date: 2026-04-01*
*Based on: competitive landscape, Reddit validation, market sizing*

## Primary ICP: The Service Freelancer

### Profile
- **Who:** Freelance web developers, designers, copywriters, marketing consultants
- **Revenue:** $50K-250K/year
- **Clients per year:** 8-30 projects
- **Pain intensity:** 🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥 (highest — they have testimonials scattered in emails, no time to transform them)
- **SaaS budget:** $50-150/mo across all tools
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium-high (value-conscious, not cheap)

### Why They're #1
1. **Highest testimonial volume** relative to their marketing capacity — they get client feedback but have zero bandwidth to turn it into content
2. **Most vocal online** — r/freelance, r/webdev, IndieHackers are full of these people expressing this exact pain
3. **Portfolio-dependent** — their next client literally comes from showcasing past work. Testimonial transformation directly drives revenue.
4. **ChatGPT workaround users** — 70% already use AI prompts for this. They've validated the workflow; we productize it.

### Behavior Patterns
- Searches: "how to get testimonials from clients," "freelance portfolio tips," "case study template freelance"
- Tools: Carrd, Notion, Canva, Testimonial.to (free), sometimes Senja
- Hangs out: r/freelance, r/webdev, IndieHackers, Freelance Twitter/X
- Buys through: ProductHunt, peer recommendations, blog content, Reddit threads
- Decision time: Fast (same day to 1 week for <$20/mo tools)

### What They'd Use ProofKit For
1. **Case studies** → Portfolio pages and proposals
2. **LinkedIn posts** → Client win announcements
3. **Website testimonial sections** → Embeddable widgets
4. **Email signatures** → Social proof in outreach

### Key Messaging
> "You just finished a great project. The client sent a glowing email. Now what? ProofKit turns that one email into a case study for your portfolio, a LinkedIn post, an embeddable widget, and an email snippet — in 30 seconds."

## Secondary ICP: The Small Agency Owner

### Profile
- **Who:** Digital marketing agencies, web dev shops, design studios (2-15 people)
- **Revenue:** $200K-2M/year
- **Clients per year:** 20-100+ projects
- **Pain intensity:** 🔥🔥🔥🔥 (high — more volume, often have a "testimonial backlog" they never process)
- **SaaS budget:** $200-500/mo
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium (ROI-focused, willing to pay for time savings)

### Why They're #2
1. **Higher volume, higher willingness to pay** — more clients = more testimonials = more value from automation
2. **Multi-client management need** — the Agency tier ($39/mo) maps perfectly
3. **Already using testimonial tools** — upgrade path from Testimonial.to/Senja (which don't generate assets)
4. **Marketing-savvy** — understand the value of repurposing content across channels

### Behavior Patterns
- Searches: "client testimonial management," "agency social proof strategy," "case study automation"
- Tools: HubSpot, Testimonial.to (paid), Senja, Canva Pro, Buffer
- Hangs out: Agency-specific Slack communities, LinkedIn, Twitter/X marketing circles
- Buys through: LinkedIn recommendations, agency peer networks, SaaS review sites (G2, Capterra)
- Decision time: 1-4 weeks (may need team buy-in)

### What They'd Use ProofKit For
1. **Case studies for each client** → Sales collateral
2. **Social media content calendar** → Automated post generation from completed projects
3. **White-label client reports** → "Here's the social proof we generated from your project"
4. **Website testimonial sections** → Client portfolio pages

## Tertiary ICP: The SaaS Founder / Product Marketer

### Profile
- **Who:** Early-stage SaaS companies (pre-Series A), product marketers at startups
- **Revenue:** $0-5M ARR
- **Users/customers:** 50-5,000
- **Pain intensity:** 🔥🔥🔥 (moderate — they have customer feedback but focus more on product than testimonial marketing)
- **SaaS budget:** $500+/mo
- **Price sensitivity:** Low (budget available, ROI matters)

### Why They're #3 (Not #1)
1. **Longer sales cycle** — more decision-makers, more evaluation
2. **Different content needs** — they want customer stories for landing pages, not freelance portfolios
3. **Often have marketing teams** — can do this manually (or already use customer.io, HubSpot, etc.)
4. **But still underserved** — even SaaS companies with marketing teams rarely repurpose testimonials effectively

### What They'd Use ProofKit For
1. **Landing page social proof blocks** → Auto-generated from NPS/review data
2. **Customer stories / case studies** → For sales enablement
3. **G2/Capterra review snippets** → Repurposed into social content
4. **Email marketing testimonial blocks** → Drip campaign social proof

## Anti-ICPs (Who We DON'T Target)

| Segment | Why Not |
|---------|---------|
| Enterprise (500+ employees) | Long sales cycles, need SOC2, custom integrations — not our game |
| E-commerce / retail | Review management (Yotpo, Judge.me) is a different market |
| Restaurants / local businesses | Google/Yelp reviews are their world — different problem |
| Gig workers (Uber, DoorDash) | No client testimonials to collect |
| Students / hobbyists | No budget, no client work |
